DSE 化學筆記：課題七 氧化還原、化學電池和電解

1. 氧化還原反應 (Redox Reactions)

氧化和還原必須同時進行。判斷氧化還原最準確的方法是計算氧化數 (Oxidation Number, ON) 的變化。

必背反應與顏色變化：
強氧化劑 酸化高錳酸鉀溶液 ($KMnO_4$)：紫色 (Purple) $\rightarrow$ 無色 (Colorless)
$MnO_4^-(aq) + 8H^+(aq) + 5e^- \rightarrow Mn^{2+}(aq) + 4H_2O(l)$
強氧化劑 酸化重鉻酸鉀溶液 ($K_2Cr_2O_7$)：橙色 (Orange) $\rightarrow$ 綠色 (Green)
$Cr_2O_7^{2-}(aq) + 14H^+(aq) + 6e^- \rightarrow 2Cr^{3+}(aq) + 7H_2O(l)$

2. 化學電池 (Chemical Cells)

將化學能轉化為電能。在由兩種不同金屬組成的電池中：

  • 較活潑的金屬（例如鋅 $Zn$）會失去電子，形成負極 (Negative pole)。此處發生氧化作用（陽極）。
    $Zn(s) \rightarrow Zn^{2+}(aq) + 2e^-$
  • 較不活潑的金屬（例如銅 $Cu$）的電極上，溶液中的陽離子會接收電子，形成正極 (Positive pole)。此處發生還原作用（陰極）。
    $Cu^{2+}(aq) + 2e^- \rightarrow Cu(s)$

鹽橋的功能：1. 完成電路； 2. 提供離子（如 $K^+, NO_3^-$）以平衡兩個半電池中積聚的電荷。

3. 電解 (Electrolysis)

將直流電通過電解質以引發非自發化學反應。優先放電 (Preferential discharge) 取決於三大因素：

  1. 電化學序 (Position in ECS)：通常位置越低，越容易放電。
  2. 濃度效應 (Concentration effect)：若鹵素離子（如 $Cl^-, Br^-, I^-$）濃度極高，它們會優先於 $OH^-$ 放電。
  3. 電極本質 (Nature of electrode)：若使用非惰性電極（如銅陽極），陽極金屬本身會優先被氧化溶解。

DSE Chemistry Notes: Topic 7 Redox, Chemical Cells & Electrolysis

1. Redox Reactions

Oxidation and reduction must occur simultaneously. The most accurate way to identify a redox reaction is to check for changes in Oxidation Number (ON).

Must-Memorize Reactions & Color Changes:
Strong oxidizing agent Acidified Potassium Permanganate ($KMnO_4$): Purple $\rightarrow$ Colorless
$MnO_4^-(aq) + 8H^+(aq) + 5e^- \rightarrow Mn^{2+}(aq) + 4H_2O(l)$
Strong oxidizing agent Acidified Potassium Dichromate ($K_2Cr_2O_7$): Orange $\rightarrow$ Green
$Cr_2O_7^{2-}(aq) + 14H^+(aq) + 6e^- \rightarrow 2Cr^{3+}(aq) + 7H_2O(l)$

2. Chemical Cells

Convert chemical energy to electrical energy. In a cell made of two different metals:

  • The more reactive metal (e.g., $Zn$) loses electrons and forms the negative pole. Oxidation occurs here (Anode).
    $Zn(s) \rightarrow Zn^{2+}(aq) + 2e^-$
  • At the less reactive metal (e.g., $Cu$), cations in the solution gain electrons, forming the positive pole. Reduction occurs here (Cathode).
    $Cu^{2+}(aq) + 2e^- \rightarrow Cu(s)$

Functions of Salt Bridge: 1. Complete the circuit; 2. Provide ions (e.g., $K^+, NO_3^-$) to balance the built-up charges in the two half-cells.

3. Electrolysis

Passing direct current through an electrolyte to induce a non-spontaneous chemical reaction. Preferential discharge depends on three factors:

  1. Position in ECS: Ions lower in the series usually discharge more readily.
  2. Concentration effect: If halide ions (e.g., $Cl^-, Br^-, I^-$) are in high concentration, they discharge preferentially over $OH^-$.
  3. Nature of electrode: If non-inert electrodes are used (e.g., Cu anode), the anode metal itself oxidises and dissolves preferentially.

Q1. 將酸化高錳酸鉀溶液 ($KMnO_4(aq)$) 加入硫酸鐵(II)溶液 ($FeSO_4(aq)$) 中。描述混合溶液預期的顏色變化，並寫出該反應的完整離子方程式。

Q1. Acidified potassium permanganate solution ($KMnO_4(aq)$) is added to iron(II) sulphate solution ($FeSO_4(aq)$). Describe the expected colour change of the mixture, and write the overall ionic equation for the reaction.

正確答案：
顏色變化：溶液由紫色變為淡黃色（或變為黃色/啡色）。

離子方程式：

Correct Answer:
Colour change: The solution changes from purple to pale yellow (or to yellow/brown).

Ionic Equation:

MnO₄⁻ (aq) + 5Fe²⁺ (aq) + 8H⁺ (aq) → Mn²⁺ (aq) + 5Fe³⁺ (aq) + 4H₂O (l)

精修解析： 高錳酸根離子 ($MnO_4^-$) 是強氧化劑，自身呈紫色，被還原後生成近乎無色的錳(II)離子 ($Mn^{2+}$)。同時，淡綠色的鐵(II)離子 ($Fe^{2+}$) 被氧化成黃色/啡色的鐵(III)離子 ($Fe^{3+}$)。兩者混合後，主導顏色會是生成的 $Fe^{3+}$ 的淡黃色。這條半方程式合併是 DSE 每年必考的熱門！

Detailed Explanation: Permanganate ion ($MnO_4^-$) is a strong oxidising agent with a purple colour, which is reduced to virtually colourless manganese(II) ions ($Mn^{2+}$). Concurrently, pale green iron(II) ions ($Fe^{2+}$) are oxidised to yellow/brown iron(III) ions ($Fe^{3+}$). When mixed, the dominant color will be the pale yellow of the formed $Fe^{3+}$. Combining these two half-equations is a perennial favorite in DSE!

Q2. 在一個由鋅（負極）和銅（正極）組成的化學電池中，兩邊的半電池由一個含有飽和硝酸鉀溶液 ($KNO_3$) 的鹽橋 (Salt bridge) 連接。簡述鹽橋的兩項主要功能，並指出電子在「外電路 (External circuit)」中的流向。

Q2. In a chemical cell consisting of zinc (negative electrode) and copper (positive electrode), the two half-cells are connected by a salt bridge containing saturated potassium nitrate solution ($KNO_3$). Briefly state TWO main functions of the salt bridge, and state the direction of electron flow in the "external circuit".

查看答案及詳細解析 View Answer and Detailed Explanation

正確答案：
鹽橋的功能：1. 接通/完成電路 (Complete the circuit)；2. 容許離子移動以維持兩邊溶液的電中性 (Maintain electrical neutrality)。
電子的流向：電子經外電路（導線）由鋅電極流向銅電極。

精修解析： 【DSE 零分地雷！】千萬不要寫「電子通過鹽橋/溶液」！在化學電池中，電子**只能**在金屬導線（外電路）中流動；而在溶液和鹽橋中，負責導電的是**可自由移動的離子**（陰離子流向負極，陽離子流向正極）。這也是 MC 題最喜歡坑學生的考點。

Correct Answer:
Functions of salt bridge: 1. To complete the circuit; 2. To maintain electrical neutrality in both solutions by allowing ions to migrate.
Direction of electron flow: Electrons flow from the zinc electrode to the copper electrode through the external circuit (wires).

Detailed Explanation: [DSE Zero-mark Landmine!] NEVER write "electrons pass through the salt bridge or solution"! In chemical cells, electrons **only** travel through the metal wires (external circuit). Inside the solutions and the salt bridge, electricity is conducted by **mobile ions** (anions migrate to the negative electrode, cations migrate to the positive electrode). This is one of the HKEAA's favorite traps in multiple-choice questions.

Q3. 使用碳（石墨）作為惰性電極，電解稀硫酸銅(II)溶液 ($CuSO_4(aq)$)。預測在陽極 (Anode) 釋出的氣體名稱，並利用電化學序 (Electrochemical series) 解釋其成因。

Q3. Dilute copper(II) sulphate solution ($CuSO_4(aq)$) is electrolysed using carbon (graphite) as inert electrodes. Predict the name of the gas liberated at the anode, and explain its formation using the electrochemical series.

查看答案及詳細解析 View Answer and Detailed Explanation

正確答案： 釋出的氣體是氧氣 (Oxygen)。

成因解析： 在電解過程中，帶負電的陰離子：硫酸根離子 ($SO_4^{2-}$) 和由水電離出來的氫氧根離子 ($OH^-$) 會被吸引到正極（陽極）。由於 $OH^-$ 離子在電化學序中的位置比 $SO_4^{2-}$ 低（或者是更強的還原劑），因此 $OH^-$ 離子會優先放電 (Preferentially discharged) 失去電子，生成氧氣和水。陽極半方程式為：$4OH^-(aq) \rightarrow O_2(g) + 2H_2O(l) + 4e^-$。

Correct Answer: The gas liberated is oxygen.

Explanation: During electrolysis, negatively charged anions, namely sulphate ions ($SO_4^{2-}$) and hydroxide ions ($OH^-$) from the auto-ionization of water, are attracted to the positive electrode (anode). Since $OH^-$ ions occupy a lower position in the electrochemical series than $SO_4^{2-}$ ions (i.e., $OH^-$ is a stronger reducing agent), $OH^-$ ions are preferentially discharged by losing electrons to form oxygen gas and water. The anode half-equation is: $4OH^-(aq) \rightarrow O_2(g) + 2H_2O(l) + 4e^-$.
