DSE 化學筆記：課題七 氧化還原、化學電池和電解

1. 氧化還原反應 (Redox Reactions)

氧化和還原必須同時進行。判斷氧化還原最準確的方法是計算氧化數 (Oxidation Number, ON) 的變化。

必背反應與顏色變化：
強氧化劑 酸化高錳酸鉀溶液 ($KMnO_4$)：紫色 (Purple) $\rightarrow$ 無色 (Colorless)
$MnO_4^-(aq) + 8H^+(aq) + 5e^- \rightarrow Mn^{2+}(aq) + 4H_2O(l)$
強氧化劑 酸化重鉻酸鉀溶液 ($K_2Cr_2O_7$)：橙色 (Orange) $\rightarrow$ 綠色 (Green)
$Cr_2O_7^{2-}(aq) + 14H^+(aq) + 6e^- \rightarrow 2Cr^{3+}(aq) + 7H_2O(l)$

2. 化學電池 (Chemical Cells)

將化學能轉化為電能。在由兩種不同金屬組成的電池中：

  • 較活潑的金屬（例如鋅 $Zn$）會失去電子，形成負極 (Negative pole)。此處發生氧化作用（陽極）。
    $Zn(s) \rightarrow Zn^{2+}(aq) + 2e^-$
  • 較不活潑的金屬（例如銅 $Cu$）的電極上，溶液中的陽離子會接收電子，形成正極 (Positive pole)。此處發生還原作用（陰極）。
    $Cu^{2+}(aq) + 2e^- \rightarrow Cu(s)$

鹽橋的功能：1. 完成電路； 2. 提供離子（如 $K^+, NO_3^-$）以平衡兩個半電池中積聚的電荷。

3. 電解 (Electrolysis)

將直流電通過電解質以引發非自發化學反應。優先放電 (Preferential discharge) 取決於三大因素：

  1. 電化學序 (Position in ECS)：通常位置越低，越容易放電。
  2. 濃度效應 (Concentration effect)：若鹵素離子（如 $Cl^-, Br^-, I^-$）濃度極高，它們會優先於 $OH^-$ 放電。
  3. 電極本質 (Nature of electrode)：若使用非惰性電極（如銅陽極），陽極金屬本身會優先被氧化溶解。

DSE Chemistry Notes: Topic 7 Redox, Chemical Cells & Electrolysis

1. Redox Reactions

Oxidation and reduction must occur simultaneously. The most accurate way to identify a redox reaction is to check for changes in Oxidation Number (ON).

Must-Memorize Reactions & Color Changes:
Strong oxidizing agent Acidified Potassium Permanganate ($KMnO_4$): Purple $\rightarrow$ Colorless
$MnO_4^-(aq) + 8H^+(aq) + 5e^- \rightarrow Mn^{2+}(aq) + 4H_2O(l)$
Strong oxidizing agent Acidified Potassium Dichromate ($K_2Cr_2O_7$): Orange $\rightarrow$ Green
$Cr_2O_7^{2-}(aq) + 14H^+(aq) + 6e^- \rightarrow 2Cr^{3+}(aq) + 7H_2O(l)$

2. Chemical Cells

Convert chemical energy to electrical energy. In a cell made of two different metals:

  • The more reactive metal (e.g., $Zn$) loses electrons and forms the negative pole. Oxidation occurs here (Anode).
    $Zn(s) \rightarrow Zn^{2+}(aq) + 2e^-$
  • At the less reactive metal (e.g., $Cu$), cations in the solution gain electrons, forming the positive pole. Reduction occurs here (Cathode).
    $Cu^{2+}(aq) + 2e^- \rightarrow Cu(s)$

Functions of Salt Bridge: 1. Complete the circuit; 2. Provide ions (e.g., $K^+, NO_3^-$) to balance the built-up charges in the two half-cells.

3. Electrolysis

Passing direct current through an electrolyte to induce a non-spontaneous chemical reaction. Preferential discharge depends on three factors:

  1. Position in ECS: Ions lower in the series usually discharge more readily.
  2. Concentration effect: If halide ions (e.g., $Cl^-, Br^-, I^-$) are in high concentration, they discharge preferentially over $OH^-$.
  3. Nature of electrode: If non-inert electrodes are used (e.g., Cu anode), the anode metal itself oxidises and dissolves preferentially.
