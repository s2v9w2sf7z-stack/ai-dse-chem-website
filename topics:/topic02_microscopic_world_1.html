DSE 化學筆記：課題二 微觀世界 I

核心概念摘要

化學鍵的本質：所有化學鍵（金屬、離子、共價）本質上都是帶相反電荷粒子之間的靜電引力。
  • 原子與同位素：同位素擁有相同數量的質子，但中子數量不同。它們的化學性質完全相同。
  • 化學鍵：
    • 金屬鍵：金屬陽離子（如 $Mg^{2+}$）與離域電子之間的引力。
    • 離子鍵：金屬與非金屬之間的電子轉移。例如鈉原子失去一個電子變成 $Na^+$，氯原子獲得一個電子變成 $Cl^-$。
    • 共價鍵：非金屬原子間透過共用電子對以達到穩定的八隅體（Octet）結構。
  • 四種結構與性質：
    • 巨型離子結構：固態不導電，熔融或水溶液狀態導電（因離子變得可自由移動）。具高熔點。
    • 簡單分子結構：低熔點/沸點，因為分子間的范德華力很弱。即使分子內共價鍵很強，狀態改變只需克服分子間引力。
    • 巨型共價結構：如鑽石和石英（$SiO_2$）。極高熔點且堅硬。石墨是例外，因擁有離域電子而能導電。

DSE Chemistry Notes: Topic 2 Microscopic World I

Core Concepts Summary

Nature of Bonding: All chemical bonds (metallic, ionic, covalent) are fundamentally electrostatic attractions between oppositely charged particles.
  • Atoms and Isotopes: Isotopes have the same number of protons but different numbers of neutrons. They have identical chemical properties.
  • Chemical Bonding:
    • Metallic bond: The attraction between metal cations (e.g., $Mg^{2+}$) and a sea of delocalised electrons.
    • Ionic bond: Electron transfer between metals and non-metals. E.g., a sodium atom loses an electron to form $Na^+$, while a chlorine atom gains one to form $Cl^-$.
    • Covalent bond: Sharing of electron pairs between non-metal atoms to attain a stable octet structure.
  • Four Structures and Properties:
    • Giant ionic structure: Non-conductors in solid state, but conduct electricity in molten or aqueous states (due to mobile ions). High melting points.
    • Simple molecular structure: Low melting/boiling points because the Van der Waals' forces between molecules are weak.
    • Giant covalent structure: e.g., Diamond and quartz ($SiO_2$). Extremely high melting points and very hard. Graphite is an exception; it conducts electricity due to delocalised electrons.

Q1. 碳-12 ($^{12}C$) 和 碳-14 ($^{14}C$) 是碳的兩種同位素。試指出它們在原子結構（質子、中子、電子數量）上的相同與不同之處。

Q1. Carbon-12 ($^{12}C$) and Carbon-14 ($^{14}C$) are two isotopes of carbon. State their similarities and differences in terms of atomic structure (number of protons, neutrons, and electrons).

查看答案及詳細解析 View Answer and Detailed Explanation

正確答案：
相同之處：兩者均擁有 6 個質子和 6 個電子。
不同之處：碳-12 有 6 個中子，而碳-14 有 8 個中子。

精修解析： 這是同位素的最基礎定義。同位素是指具有相同質子數（因此屬於同一種元素且化學性質相同），但中子數不同（質量數不同）的原子。DSE MC 題經常以此概念作變奏考核。

Correct Answer:
Similarities: Both have 6 protons and 6 electrons.
Differences: Carbon-12 has 6 neutrons, whereas Carbon-14 has 8 neutrons.

Detailed Explanation: This is the fundamental definition of isotopes. Isotopes are atoms of the same element with the same number of protons (hence identical chemical properties) but different numbers of neutrons (different mass numbers). This concept is frequently tested in DSE multiple-choice questions.

Q2. 解釋為何固體氯化鈉 ($NaCl(s)$) 不能導電，但熔融的氯化鈉 ($NaCl(l)$) 卻能導電。

Q2. Explain why solid sodium chloride ($NaCl(s)$) cannot conduct electricity, but molten sodium chloride ($NaCl(l)$) can conduct electricity.

查看答案及詳細解析 View Answer and Detailed Explanation

正確答案： 在固態時，氯化鈉擁有巨型離子結構，離子被緊緊固定在晶格的固定位置中，不能自由移動。在熔融狀態時，離子鍵被打破，氯化鈉具有可自由移動的離子 (mobile ions) 來導電。

精修解析： 【DSE 0分陷阱警告！】在解釋離子化合物的導電性時，帶電荷的粒子絕對是「離子 (ions)」。如果寫了「可自由移動的電子 (mobile electrons)」，會被考評局直接扣去全部分數！

Correct Answer: In the solid state, sodium chloride has a giant ionic structure where ions are tightly held in fixed positions in the lattice and cannot move freely. In the molten state, the ionic bonds are broken, and sodium chloride contains mobile ions to conduct electricity.

Detailed Explanation: [DSE Zero-mark Trap Alert!] When explaining the electrical conductivity of ionic compounds, the charge carriers are "ions". If you write "mobile electrons", the HKEAA will deduct all marks directly!

Q3. 鑽石 (Diamond) 和石墨 (Graphite) 均為碳的同素異形體，擁有巨型共價結構。利用原子結構的知識，解釋為何石墨能導電，而鑽石則不能。

Q3. Diamond and graphite are allotropes of carbon, and both possess a giant covalent structure. Using knowledge of atomic structure, explain why graphite can conduct electricity whereas diamond cannot.

查看答案及詳細解析 View Answer and Detailed Explanation

正確答案： 在石墨中，每個碳原子只與另外三個碳原子形成共價鍵，剩下的一個最外層電子成為離域電子 (delocalised electron)，能在層與層之間自由移動，因此能導電。
在鑽石中，每個碳原子與另外四個碳原子形成共價鍵，所有最外層電子均參與鍵合，沒有離域電子，因此不能導電。

精修解析： 這是考評局極度喜歡的經典 3 分題。答題的結構必須對稱：先講石墨有「離域電子」，再講鑽石「所有電子都參與鍵合（或沒有離域電子）」，這樣才能全取滿分。

Correct Answer: In graphite, each carbon atom is covalently bonded to only three other carbon atoms, leaving one outermost electron as a delocalised electron (which is free to move between the layers), making it an electrical conductor.
In diamond, each carbon atom is covalently bonded to four other carbon atoms. All outermost electrons are involved in bonding, leaving no delocalised electrons, hence it cannot conduct electricity.

Detailed Explanation: This is a classic 3-mark question highly favored by the HKEAA. Your answer must be structured symmetrically: first explain that graphite has "delocalised electrons", then explain that in diamond "all electrons are involved in bonding (or there are no delocalised electrons)" to secure full marks.
