DSE 化學筆記：課題十二 化學世界中的規律

1. 第三週期元素的熔點變化 (Melting Point Trend)

熔點變化完全取決於元素的結構與鍵合：

元素結構 (Structure)熔點高低與原因
$Na, Mg, Al$巨型金屬結構高。須克服金屬陽離子與離域電子間的強大金屬鍵。
$Si$ (硅)巨型共價結構極高（最高點）。須克服原子間大量的強大共價鍵。
$P, S, Cl$簡單分子結構低。只需克服分子間微弱的范德華力。
DSE 必考陷阱：比較 P, S, Cl 的熔點
這三者的真實分子形態為：$P_4$、$S_8$、$Cl_2$。分子大小（電子數量）的順序為：$S_8 > P_4 > Cl_2$。分子越大，范德華力越強，因此熔點順序為：$S > P > Cl$。

2. 第三週期氧化物的酸鹼性

隨着原子序增加，氧化物由鹼性過渡到酸性：

  • 鹼性 (Basic)：$Na_2O, MgO$（能與酸反應）。
  • 兩性 (Amphoteric)：$Al_2O_3$（既與酸反應，也與鹼反應）。
  • 酸性 (Acidic)：$SiO_2, P_4O_{10}, SO_2, SO_3, Cl_2O$（能與鹼反應）。

3. 過渡金屬的一般性質 (Transition Metals)

過渡金屬（如鐵 $Fe$、銅 $Cu$、錳 $Mn$）除了擁有高熔點和高密度的物理性質外，還具備以下四大獨特化學性質（主族金屬通常不具備）：
  1. 具有可變的氧化態 (Variable oxidation states)：例如鐵有 +2 ($Fe^{2+}$) 和 +3 ($Fe^{3+}$)；因為它們的 3d 和 4s 電子層能量相近。
  2. 形成有色離子 (Coloured ions)：過渡金屬的化合物多帶有顏色。例如 $Cu^{2+}(aq)$ 呈藍色，$MnO_4^-(aq)$ 呈紫色。
  3. 展現催化性質 (Catalytic properties)：過渡金屬及其化合物是優良的催化劑。例如哈柏法使用鐵 ($Fe$)，分解雙氧水使用二氧化錳 ($MnO_2$)。
  4. 形成錯離子 (Complex formation)：金屬陽離子能與配位體（如水分子或氨分子）結合形成錯合物，如 $[Cu(H_2O)_6]^{2+}$。

DSE Chemistry Notes: Topic 12 Patterns in the Chemical World

1. Trend in Melting Points of Period 3 Elements

The variation in melting points depends entirely on the structure and bonding:

ElementsStructureMelting Point & Reason
$Na, Mg, Al$Giant metallic structureHigh. Needs to overcome strong metallic bonds.
$Si$ (Silicon)Giant covalent structureVery high (Peak). Needs to break numerous strong covalent bonds.
$P, S, Cl$Simple molecular structureLow. Needs to overcome only weak Van der Waals' forces.
DSE Trap: Comparing melting points of P, S, Cl
Their actual molecular forms are: $P_4$, $S_8$, $Cl_2$. The order of molecular size is: $S_8 > P_4 > Cl_2$. Larger molecules have stronger Van der Waals' forces, so the melting point order is: $S > P > Cl$.

2. Acid-base Nature of Period 3 Oxides

Across the period, oxides change from basic to acidic:

  • Basic: $Na_2O, MgO$ (React with acids).
  • Amphoteric: $Al_2O_3$ (Reacts with BOTH acids and alkalis).
  • Acidic: $SiO_2, P_4O_{10}, SO_2, SO_3, Cl_2O$ (React with alkalis).

3. General Properties of Transition Metals

Transition metals (e.g., $Fe, Cu, Mn$) have high melting points and densities. Compared to main group metals, they exhibit four unique chemical properties:
  1. Variable oxidation states: e.g., Iron forms +2 ($Fe^{2+}$) and +3 ($Fe^{3+}$) because their 3d and 4s subshells are close in energy.
  2. Coloured ions: Compounds are often coloured in solid state or aqueous solution. E.g., $Cu^{2+}(aq)$ is blue, $MnO_4^-(aq)$ is purple.
  3. Catalytic properties: Widely used as catalysts. E.g., Iron ($Fe$) in the Haber process; Manganese(IV) oxide ($MnO_2$) in the decomposition of $H_2O_2$.
  4. Complex formation: Metal cations can bond with ligands (e.g., $H_2O, NH_3$) to form complex ions, such as $[Cu(H_2O)_6]^{2+}$.

Q1. 橫跨元素週期表第三週期（從鈉 $Na$ 到氬 $Ar$），元素的原子半徑（Atomic radius）呈現什麼變化規律？試從微觀結構的角度詳細解釋原因。

Q1. Across Period 3 of the Periodic Table (from sodium $Na$ to argon $Ar$), what is the trend in atomic radius of the elements? Explain this trend in terms of microscopic structure.

查看答案及詳細解析 View Answer and Detailed Explanation

正確答案： 原子半徑逐漸減少 (Decreases)。

原因解析：
1. 從左到右，原子的原子序數（或核電荷/質子數目）逐漸增加。
2. 然而，各元素的電子均被填入相同的最外電子層，因此內層電子的屏蔽效應 (Shielding effect) 保持大致相同。
3. 結果，原子核對最外層電子的淨吸引力（有效核電荷，Effective nuclear charge）逐漸增強，將電子層向內拉得更近，導致原子半徑減少。

精修解析： 【DSE 滿分三步曲】解釋週期表規律是必考長問答。拿滿分必須包含三個層次：1. 點名「核電荷/質子數增加」；2. 指出「電子層數相同/屏蔽效應相若」；3. 結論「有效核電荷增加/引力變強」。漏掉任何一步都會被扣 1 分！特別注意：答題時不可只寫「吸引力變大」，必須明確是「**原子核對最外層電子的吸引力**」。

Correct Answer: The atomic radius gradually decreases.

Explanation:
1. From left to right, the atomic number (or nuclear charge / number of protons) increases gradually.
2. However, the outermost electrons are added to the same electron shell, so the shielding effect provided by inner-shell electrons remains roughly constant.
3. Consequently, the net attractive force exerted by the nucleus on the outermost electrons (effective nuclear charge) increases gradually, pulling the electron shells closer to the nucleus and resulting in a smaller atomic radius.

Detailed Explanation: [DSE 3-Step Success Formula] Explaining periodic trends is a structured long question. Full marks require three components: 1. State "nuclear charge/proton number increases"; 2. State "same number of electron shells / similar shielding effect"; 3. Conclude "effective nuclear charge increases / attraction gets stronger". Omitting any step results in mark deduction! Note: Never just write "attraction increases", it must be "the attraction **exerted by the nucleus on the outermost electrons**".

Q2. 第三週期元素中，矽 ($Si$) 的熔點非常高，而磷 ($P_4$) 的熔點卻大幅度下降。試從這兩種元素的結構和化學鍵（鍵型）的角度，解釋這個巨大的物理性質差異。

Q2. Among Period 3 elements, silicon ($Si$) has a very high melting point, whereas the melting point of phosphorus ($P_4$) drops drastically. Explain this massive difference in physical properties in terms of the structures and bonding of the two elements.

查看答案及詳細解析 View Answer and Detailed Explanation

正確答案：
1. 矽具有巨型共價結構 (Giant covalent structure)。熔化矽時，必須克服原子之間強大的共價鍵 (Covalent bonds)，這需要極大量的熱能，因此熔點極高。
2. 磷具有簡單分子結構 (Simple molecular structure)。熔化磷時，只需要克服分子之間微弱的范德華力 (Van der Waals' forces)，所需的熱能很少，因此熔點較低。

精修解析： 【結構與鍵型對比】這是 DSE 最常考的對比題型。考生常見的失分點在於混淆了「克服的力」。請牢記：簡單分子（如 $P_4, S_8, Cl_2$）在熔化或沸騰時，**分子內部的共價鍵是完全沒有被破壞的**，改變的只是分子之間的距離，所以克服的是「分子間引力（范德華力）」。答題時，結構名稱（Giant vs Simple）與引力名稱（Covalent bond vs Van der Waals' forces）必須完美配對。

Correct Answer:
1. Silicon has a giant covalent structure. To melt silicon, strong covalent bonds between atoms must be broken, which requires a huge amount of heat energy, resulting in a very high melting point.
2. Phosphorus has a simple molecular structure. To melt phosphorus, only the weak Van der Waals' forces between molecules need to be overcome, which requires very little heat energy, resulting in a low melting point.

Detailed Explanation: [Structure and Bonding Comparison] This is a core comparison question in DSE. The most common mistake is confusing "which force is overcome". Keep in mind that for simple molecules (like $P_4, S_8, Cl_2$), the **internal covalent bonds are NOT broken during melting or boiling**; only the distances between molecules change, so they overcome "intermolecular forces (Van der Waals' forces)". In your exam, structural types (Giant vs Simple) and force types (Covalent bond vs Van der Waals' forces) must match perfectly.

Q3. 鐵 ($Fe$) 是一種過渡金屬（Transition metal）。列舉過渡金屬有別於第三週期典型金屬（如鈉、鎂）的兩項典型特徵（化學或物理性質均可）。

Q3. Iron ($Fe$) is a transition metal. State TWO characteristic properties of transition metals that distinguish them from typical Period 3 metals (such as sodium and magnesium).

查看答案及詳細解析 View Answer and Detailed Explanation

正確答案：（任選其中兩項）
1. 具有可變的氧化態 (Variable oxidation states)（例如鐵有 $+2$ 和 $+3$ 氧化態）。
2. 能形成有色的離子/化合物 (Coloured ions/compounds)（例如 $Fe^{2+}$ 呈淡綠色，$Fe^{3+}$ 呈黃/啡色）。
3. 元素或其化合物常可作為催化劑 (Catalysts)（例如哈柏法中用鐵作催化劑）。
4. 具有較高的密度、熔點和硬度（因為其金屬鍵較強）。

精修解析： 這是過渡金屬性質的直問記憶題。DSE 考生常犯的錯誤是寫「能導電導熱」或「有延展性」——這些是**所有金屬**（包括主族金屬鈉、鎂、鈣等）都具備的共同通性，無法用來體現過渡金屬的「獨特性」，因此不會得分。必須鎖定「可變氧化態」、「有色化合物」或「催化性質」這些專屬特徵才安全！

Correct Answer: (Any two of the following)
1. They exhibit variable oxidation states (e.g., iron has $+2$ and $+3$ oxidation states).
2. They form coloured ions/compounds (e.g., $Fe^{2+}$ is pale green, $Fe^{3+}$ is yellow/brown).
3. The elements or their compounds often act as catalysts (e.g., iron is used as a catalyst in the Haber Process).
4. They have higher densities, melting points, and hardness (due to stronger metallic bonding).

Detailed Explanation: This is a recall question testing the unique characteristics of transition metals. A common blunder is writing "can conduct electricity/heat" or "is malleable"—these are general properties shared by **all metals** (including main-group metals like $Na, Mg, Ca$), so they cannot show the "uniqueness" of transition metals and earn zero marks. You must lock on to specific transition properties like "variable oxidation states", "coloured compounds", or "catalytic properties" to score!
