DSE 化學筆記：課題十 化學平衡

1. 動態平衡的兩大特徵

在一個密閉系統中，當達到動態平衡時：
1. 正向反應速率 = 逆向反應速率 (Rate of forward reaction = Rate of backward reaction)。
2. 系統中所有反應物和生成物的濃度保持不變。

2. 平衡常數 ($K_c$)

對於反應： $aA(aq) + bB(aq) \rightleftharpoons cC(aq) + dD(aq)$

$$K_c = \frac{[C]^c[D]^d}{[A]^a[B]^b}$$
DSE 必考陷阱：
1. 寫 $K_c$ 表達式時，純固體 $(s)$ 和純液體 $(l)$（如作為溶劑的水）的濃度不包括在內。
2. 只有溫度的改變會改變 $K_c$ 的數值。濃度、壓強或加入催化劑都不會改變 $K_c$。

3. 勒沙得利爾原理 (Le Chatelier's Principle)

當系統受到改變時，平衡位置會移動以抵消該改變 (oppose the change)。

干擾因素系統反應 (平衡移動方向)
增加反應物濃度向右移動（以消耗反應物）。
增加壓強（減小體積）向氣體分子總數較少的一方移動。
升高溫度向吸熱反應 (Endothermic, $\Delta H > 0$) 的方向移動。
加入催化劑不移動。（只會令系統更快達到平衡）

4. 哈柏法 (Haber Process) 製氨

反應： $N_2(g) + 3H_2(g) \rightleftharpoons 2NH_3(g) \quad \Delta H = -92 \, kJ \, mol^{-1}$

由於這是一個正向為放熱、且正向氣體分子數目減少的反應。理論上低溫高壓能獲得最高產量。但為了平衡反應速率及生產成本，工業上採用：400-500°C、200 大氣壓、鐵催化劑的折衷條件。

DSE Chemistry Notes: Topic 10 Chemical Equilibrium

1. Two Characteristics of Dynamic Equilibrium

In a closed system, when dynamic equilibrium is reached:
1. Rate of forward reaction = Rate of backward reaction.
2. The concentrations of all reactants and products remain constant.

2. Equilibrium Constant ($K_c$)

For the reaction: $aA(aq) + bB(aq) \rightleftharpoons cC(aq) + dD(aq)$

$$K_c = \frac{[C]^c[D]^d}{[A]^a[B]^b}$$
DSE Traps:
1. Concentrations of pure solids $(s)$ and pure liquids $(l)$ (e.g., water as a solvent) are not included in the $K_c$ expression.
2. ONLY Temperature can change the value of $K_c$. Changes in concentration, pressure, or adding a catalyst do NOT change $K_c$.

3. Le Chatelier's Principle

When a system at equilibrium is subjected to a change, the equilibrium position shifts to oppose the change.

DisturbanceSystem's Response (Direction of Shift)
Increase concentration of reactantShifts to the right (to consume the reactant).
Increase pressure (Decrease volume)Shifts to the side with fewer moles of gas molecules.
Increase temperatureShifts to the endothermic direction ($\Delta H > 0$).
Add a catalystNo shift. (Only helps reach equilibrium faster).

4. Haber Process (Production of Ammonia)

Reaction: $N_2(g) + 3H_2(g) \rightleftharpoons 2NH_3(g) \quad \Delta H = -92 \, kJ \, mol^{-1}$

Since the forward reaction is exothermic and proceeds with a decrease in gas molecules, low temperature and high pressure theoretically give the maximum yield. However, considering reaction rate and cost, industry uses compromise conditions: 400-500°C, 200 atm, and an Iron catalyst.
