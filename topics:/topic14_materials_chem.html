DSE 化學筆記：課題十四 物料化學

1. 縮合聚合作用 (Condensation Polymerisation)

與加成聚合不同，縮合聚合需要單體最少具有兩個官能基，並在結合時釋出如 $H_2O$ 或 $HCl$ 等細小分子。

聚酯 (Polyester, 如 PET) 的生成： 二酸 + 二醇

$$n \, HOOC-R-COOH + n \, HO-R'-OH \rightarrow -[-OC-R-CO-O-R'-O-]-_n + 2n \, H_2O$$

聚酰胺 (Polyamide, 如 Nylon, Kevlar) 的生成： 二酸 + 二胺

$$n \, HOOC-R-COOH + n \, H_2N-R'-NH_2 \rightarrow -[-OC-R-CO-NH-R'-NH-]-_n + 2n \, H_2O$$

2. 結構如何決定塑膠的物理性質？

  • 拉伸強度 (Tensile Strength)：若聚合物直鏈能緊密排列，並在鏈間形成強大的氫鍵 (Hydrogen bonds)（如 Kevlar 中的酰胺基），其強度會極高。
  • 交聯 (Cross-linking)：引入共價鍵把聚合物鏈綁在一起。適度交聯產生彈性（如硫化橡膠）；大量交聯則形成堅硬的熱固性塑膠 (Thermosetting plastics)，受熱不熔化。
  • 塑化劑 (Plasticizer)：分子夾在聚合物鏈之間，削弱了鏈與鏈之間的范德華力。使得鏈層更容易滑動，從而把剛硬的塑膠變得柔軟。

3. 納米物料 (Nanomaterials)

DSE 常考概念：為何納米物料如此特別？
當物質被縮小到 1-100 nm（納米）時，其表面積對體積比例 (Surface area to volume ratio) 會急劇上升。這意味著極高比例的原子暴露在表面，令其化學反應性（如催化能力）大增。例如：碳納米管 (Carbon nanotubes) 由於其獨特的圓柱形石墨烯網絡，具備極高的力學強度和導電性。

DSE Chemistry Notes: Topic 14 Materials Chemistry

1. Condensation Polymerisation

Unlike addition polymerisation, condensation requires monomers with at least two functional groups and involves the elimination of a small molecule like $H_2O$ or $HCl$.

Formation of Polyester (e.g., PET): Dicarboxylic acid + Diol

$$n \, HOOC-R-COOH + n \, HO-R'-OH \rightarrow -[-OC-R-CO-O-R'-O-]-_n + 2n \, H_2O$$

Formation of Polyamide (e.g., Nylon, Kevlar): Dicarboxylic acid + Diamine

$$n \, HOOC-R-COOH + n \, H_2N-R'-NH_2 \rightarrow -[-OC-R-CO-NH-R'-NH-]-_n + 2n \, H_2O$$

2. How Structure Determines Physical Properties?

  • Tensile Strength: If polymer chains pack closely and form strong hydrogen bonds between chains (e.g., amide groups in Kevlar), the strength is exceptionally high.
  • Cross-linking: Introduction of covalent bonds linking polymer chains together. Extensive cross-linking forms rigid thermosetting plastics which do not melt upon heating.
  • Plasticizer: Small molecules embedded between polymer chains that weaken the intermolecular forces (Van der Waals' forces) between the chains. This allows chains to slide over each other easily, making a rigid plastic flexible.

3. Nanomaterials

DSE Frequently Tested Concept: Why are nanomaterials special?
When materials are reduced to 1-100 nm, their surface area to volume ratio increases dramatically. A very high proportion of atoms are exposed on the surface, greatly enhancing their chemical reactivity (e.g., as catalysts). For instance, Carbon Nanotubes (CNTs), due to their unique cylindrical graphene network, possess extremely high mechanical strength and electrical conductivity.
