DSE 化學筆記：課題十四 物料化學

1. 縮合聚合作用 (Condensation Polymerisation)

與加成聚合不同，縮合聚合需要單體最少具有兩個官能基，並在結合時釋出如 $H_2O$ 或 $HCl$ 等細小分子。

聚酯 (Polyester, 如 PET) 的生成： 二酸 + 二醇

$$n \, HOOC-R-COOH + n \, HO-R'-OH \rightarrow -[-OC-R-CO-O-R'-O-]-_n + 2n \, H_2O$$

聚酰胺 (Polyamide, 如 Nylon, Kevlar) 的生成： 二酸 + 二胺

$$n \, HOOC-R-COOH + n \, H_2N-R'-NH_2 \rightarrow -[-OC-R-CO-NH-R'-NH-]-_n + 2n \, H_2O$$

2. 結構如何決定塑膠的物理性質？

  • 拉伸強度 (Tensile Strength)：若聚合物直鏈能緊密排列，並在鏈間形成強大的氫鍵 (Hydrogen bonds)（如 Kevlar 中的酰胺基），其強度會極高。
  • 交聯 (Cross-linking)：引入共價鍵把聚合物鏈綁在一起。適度交聯產生彈性（如硫化橡膠）；大量交聯則形成堅硬的熱固性塑膠 (Thermosetting plastics)，受熱不熔化。
  • 塑化劑 (Plasticizer)：分子夾在聚合物鏈之間，削弱了鏈與鏈之間的范德華力。使得鏈層更容易滑動，從而把剛硬的塑膠變得柔軟。

3. 納米物料 (Nanomaterials)

DSE 常考概念：為何納米物料如此特別？
當物質被縮小到 1-100 nm（納米）時，其表面積對體積比例 (Surface area to volume ratio) 會急劇上升。這意味著極高比例的原子暴露在表面，令其化學反應性（如催化能力）大增。例如：碳納米管 (Carbon nanotubes) 由於其獨特的圓柱形石墨烯網絡，具備極高的力學強度和導電性。

DSE Chemistry Notes: Topic 14 Materials Chemistry

1. Condensation Polymerisation

Unlike addition polymerisation, condensation requires monomers with at least two functional groups and involves the elimination of a small molecule like $H_2O$ or $HCl$.

Formation of Polyester (e.g., PET): Dicarboxylic acid + Diol

$$n \, HOOC-R-COOH + n \, HO-R'-OH \rightarrow -[-OC-R-CO-O-R'-O-]-_n + 2n \, H_2O$$

Formation of Polyamide (e.g., Nylon, Kevlar): Dicarboxylic acid + Diamine

$$n \, HOOC-R-COOH + n \, H_2N-R'-NH_2 \rightarrow -[-OC-R-CO-NH-R'-NH-]-_n + 2n \, H_2O$$

2. How Structure Determines Physical Properties?

  • Tensile Strength: If polymer chains pack closely and form strong hydrogen bonds between chains (e.g., amide groups in Kevlar), the strength is exceptionally high.
  • Cross-linking: Introduction of covalent bonds linking polymer chains together. Extensive cross-linking forms rigid thermosetting plastics which do not melt upon heating.
  • Plasticizer: Small molecules embedded between polymer chains that weaken the intermolecular forces (Van der Waals' forces) between the chains. This allows chains to slide over each other easily, making a rigid plastic flexible.

3. Nanomaterials

DSE Frequently Tested Concept: Why are nanomaterials special?
When materials are reduced to 1-100 nm, their surface area to volume ratio increases dramatically. A very high proportion of atoms are exposed on the surface, greatly enhancing their chemical reactivity (e.g., as catalysts). For instance, Carbon Nanotubes (CNTs), due to their unique cylindrical graphene network, possess extremely high mechanical strength and electrical conductivity.

Q1. 高密度聚乙烯（HDPE）和低密度聚乙烯（LDPE）都是由乙烯單體經聚合而成。試從它們的「高聚物鏈結構（Polymer chain structure）」及「分子間引力」的角度，解釋為什麼 HDPE 的熔點和硬度都比 LDPE 高？

Q1. Both High-Density Polyethylene (HDPE) and Low-Density Polyethylene (LDPE) are polymerised from ethene monomers. In terms of their "polymer chain structure" and "intermolecular forces", explain why HDPE has a higher melting point and hardness than LDPE.

查看答案及詳細解析 View Answer and Detailed Explanation

正確答案：
1. 鏈結構方面： HDPE 的高聚物鏈主要是直鏈，分支極少；而 LDPE 的高聚物鏈則具有高度的分支（Branched）結構。
2. 排列與引力： 由於 HDPE 鏈呈線性且平直，高聚物鏈之間能夠更緊密地堆積（Pack closely together），形成較高的結晶度。相反，LDPE 的分支結構阻礙了鏈的緊密堆積。
3. 能量比較： 因此，HDPE 鏈之間的范德華力（Van der Waals' forces）較強，需要更多的熱能才能克服，使其熔點更高、結構更硬。

精修解析： 【DSE 核心大題】這是物料化學中最經典的「結構決定性質」題型。拿滿分的邏輯鏈非常嚴密：**有沒有分支 $\rightarrow$ 能否緊密堆積 $\rightarrow$ 范德華力強弱 $\rightarrow$ 所需熱能（物理性質）**。很多同學只寫「HDPE 密度高所以引力強」，漏掉了最根本的「分支結構與堆積程度（Packing）」的微觀解釋，會被考評局扣分。

Correct Answer:
1. Chain structure: HDPE consists of largely linear polymer chains with very little branching, whereas LDPE has a highly branched chain structure.
2. Packing & attraction: Because HDPE chains are linear and straight, they can pack closely together, resulting in a higher crystallinity. Conversely, the extensive branching in LDPE prevents tight packing.
3. Energy comparison: Consequently, the Van der Waals' forces between HDPE chains are much stronger and require more heat energy to overcome, giving HDPE a higher melting point and greater hardness.

Detailed Explanation: [Core DSE Question] This is a textbook example of the "structure-property relationship" in materials chemistry. The full-mark logical chain is rigorous: **Presence of branching $\rightarrow$ Capability of close packing $\rightarrow$ Strength of Van der Waals' forces $\rightarrow$ Heat energy required (physical properties)**. Many students simply jump to "HDPE has higher density so stronger forces", omitting the fundamental microscopic explanation of branching and packing, which leads to mark deduction.

Q2. 描述「熱塑性高聚物（Thermoplastic polymer）」與「熱固性高聚物（Thermosetting polymer）」在受熱時的行為差異，並從它們內部是否存在「交聯（Cross-linking）」結構來解釋這一差異。

Q2. Describe the difference in behavior when heated between a "thermoplastic polymer" and a "thermosetting polymer", and explain this difference based on the presence of "cross-linking" in their structures.

查看答案及詳細解析 View Answer and Detailed Explanation

正確答案：
行為差異：熱塑性塑膠受熱時會軟化及熔化，冷卻後硬化，且可重複塑形；熱固性塑膠受熱時不會軟化或熔化，過熱時只會直接分解（焦化）。
結構原因：
1. 熱塑性塑膠鏈與鏈之間只靠弱范德華力結合，沒有共價交聯鍵，受熱時鏈能互相滑動。
2. 熱固性塑膠鏈與鏈之間存在著強大的共價交聯鍵（Covalent cross-links），形成巨大的三維網狀結構。受熱時，這些強大的共價鍵鎖定了鏈的位置，使其無法自由滑動，因此不會軟化。

精修解析： DSE 必背關鍵字是「共價交聯鍵（Covalent cross-links）」和「三維網狀結構（Three-dimensional network）」。在 MC 題中，考評局也常用這個概念來考學生：例如聚氯乙烯（PVC）是熱塑性，而尿素甲醛（Urea-formaldehyde）則是典型的熱固性。記住，有沒有「交聯」是區分它們的唯一分水嶺。

Correct Answer:
Behavioral difference: Thermoplastics soften and melt upon heating, harden upon cooling, and can be remolded repeatedly. Thermosetting polymers do not soften or melt upon heating; they decompose (char) directly if overheated.
Structural reason:
1. Thermoplastics are held together only by weak Van der Waals' forces between chains without cross-links, allowing the chains to slide past each other when heated.
2. Thermosetting polymers contain strong covalent cross-links between polymer chains, binding them into a giant three-dimensional network. Upon heating, these strong covalent bonds lock the chain positions, preventing them from sliding, hence they do not soften.

Detailed Explanation: The mandatory DSE keywords here are "covalent cross-links" and "three-dimensional network". In multiple-choice questions, examiners love to test this classification: for example, polyvinyl chloride (PVC) is a thermoplastic, while urea-formaldehyde is a classic thermosetting polymer. Remember, the presence of "cross-linking" is the definitive dividing line.

Q3. 納米物料（Nanomaterials）通常是指尺寸在 $1 \, nm$ 至 $100 \, nm$ 之間的物質。當某種物質（例如二氧化鈦 $TiO_2$）的顆粒縮小至納米尺度時，其「表面積至體積比率（Surface area-to-volume ratio）」會發生什麼變化？這項微觀變化如何令納米 $TiO_2$ 在防曬霜中表現出比普通 $TiO_2$ 更優異的化學/物理特性？

Q3. Nanomaterials generally refer to substances with dimensions between $1 \, nm$ and $100 \, nm$. When a substance (such as titanium dioxide, $TiO_2$) is reduced to the nanoscale, what happens to its "surface area-to-volume ratio"? How does this microscopic change allow nano-$TiO_2$ to exhibit superior chemical/physical properties in sunscreens compared to bulk $TiO_2$?

查看答案及詳細解析 View Answer and Detailed Explanation

正確答案：
比率變化：表面積至體積比率會大幅度增加（Increases drastically）。
特性優勢：
1. 光學性質（物理）： 納米 $TiO_2$ 的顆粒小於可見光的波長，因此它對可見光透明（Transparent），塗在皮膚上不會留下普通防曬霜的顯眼白斑，但它依然能有效吸收和反射紫外線（UV light）。
2. 催化/化學活性： 巨大的表面積比率讓暴露在表面的原子數目大增，使其展現出極高的光催化活性（能分解皮膚表面的污染物）。

精修解析： 【DSE 新型物料熱門點】考評局在考核納米物料時，基本「送分句」就是**表面積至體積比率顯著增大**。而納米 $TiO_2$ 應用在防曬霜的例子在近年卷二中非常高頻，關鍵在於理解「為什麼它是透明的卻能防護 UV」：因為它太小了，可見光直接穿過（顯現透明），但對波長極短的有害紫外線卻能完美阻擋。

Correct Answer:
Ratio change: The surface area-to-volume ratio increases drastically.
Property advantages:
1. Optical property (Physical): Nano-$TiO_2$ particles are smaller than the wavelengths of visible light, making them transparent on the skin without leaving the conspicuous white chalky residue of bulk sunscreens. However, they remain highly effective at absorbing and scattering ultraviolet (UV) light.
2. Catalytic/Chemical activity: The enormous surface area ratio means a much higher proportion of atoms are exposed at the surface, giving it exceptional photocatalytic activity to break down pollutants.

Detailed Explanation: [DSE Modern Materials Trend] Whenever the HKEAA tests nanomaterials, the foundational scoring phrase is **a dramatic increase in the surface area-to-volume ratio**. The example of nano-$TiO_2$ in sunscreens is highly frequent in recent Paper 2 exams. The key is understanding why it is transparent yet protective: because its particles are small enough for visible light to pass through (hence transparent) but perfectly scaled to scatter or absorb short-wavelength, harmful UV rays.
